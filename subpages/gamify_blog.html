How gamification can be a powerful learning tool

What makes gamification special ?
Gamification turns tough concepts into approachable challenges by weaving them into familiar, game-like experiences. With a tight reward-feedback loop, users earn small wins as they work toward a bigger goalâ€”keeping motivation high.

Because games are interactive, they strengthen learning by letting users actively apply knowledge and get instant feedback. This converts frustrating and cumbersome activities in enjoyable and nourishing experiences. For example take a language app such as duolingo, it is an extremely gamified language learning app; We can see this constantly in it's use of sound effects, reward animations and use of video game centric mechanics such as lives and points. This keeps user retention high and is one of the core values to duolingo's success. However whilst retention is important how good is the quality of learning for the end user ?

Gamification isn't perfect, and here's why
Gamification presents itself as a brilliant solution for keeping learners engaged, however it's critical flaw is that by making things easier and reductive we can lose the inherant substance of what we are learning; This can lead to learners having shallow roots in their knowledge, this is because the struggle and effort put into an activity correlates with our braining growing new pathways for said activity. but this isn't null and void, given the correct execution and by balancing the level of gamification, we can get the best of both worlds. We can use gamification to increase retention, build consistency and break down complex topics into digestable segments whilst using more traditional methods such as writing, quizzing and studying to form a solid foundation for a user.

In conclusion, Gamification is a powerful tool that needs the correct execution as whilst it can nurture a perfect environment for learning it can also trivalise and reduce the importance of the subject matter causing no meaningful connections to be made between the brain and the content that is being studied.

  • The Bezier Method - Is a wondefully fun way to learn the generic bezier tool found across many vector applications, Find yourself making patterns and all sorts shapes whilst keeping the amount of strokes as low as possible

  • Learn Git Branching - As the name suggests, is a way to learn git branching in a nice illustrative environment where messing up does not mean deleting your entire production codebase, it also covers some more advanced topcis which can be confusing to intuit.
